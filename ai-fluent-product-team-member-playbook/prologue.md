## Prologue

**Read This First: You’re Not Late and You’re Not Crazy**

Welcome to the epic shift in the way we work, what I identified as **“The Great Inversion.”** If you’re reading this, you’ve probably had at least one “Am I already behind?” moment about AI in your work. Maybe it was the day someone dropped a slick demo of an “autonomous agent” in your team channel. Maybe it was a VP announcing in an all‑hands that “AI is our number one strategic priority.” Maybe it was a friend telling you they got promoted because they’re “the AI person” now.

And if you’re honest, your inner monologue might sound something like this:

- “I’m supposed to keep the team focused, not chase shiny objects… but this particular shiny object won’t shut up.”
- “I’m not a data scientist. I’m a PO/PM/SM/BA. Is that… still allowed?”
- “What if this actually is a big deal and I end up the person who didn’t adapt?”

You’re not late, and you’re not crazy.

You are in the awkward middle:

- The **hype** has already arrived.
- The **real, boring, practical wins** are just starting to show up in normal teams.
- The **sober, grown‑up patterns** for using AI and agents responsibly are still forming.

That awkward middle is exactly where product owners, product managers, scrum masters, business analysts, and other business‑side product people tend to live. You’re used to:

- Working in constraints that weren’t designed by you.
- Translating between “what the business thinks it wants” and “what the team can actually build.”
- Being accountable for outcomes with less authority than you’d like.

Now you’re doing all of that with AI and agents in the mix.

This playbook starts from three beliefs:

- **Your role still matters.**
AI will get better at pattern‑matching, drafting, and even some decision support; it is still terrible at owning consequences, relationships, and meaning.
- **You don’t need to become a machine learning engineer.**
You need enough AI fluency to design workflows, ask better questions, spot risk, and connect tools to outcomes. That’s a product skillset, not a research one.
- **You don’t have time for a big transformation program.**
You need practices you can insert into the work you already do: discovery, planning, delivery, monitoring, and the informal glue in between. ​

So this book doesn’t try to make you an “AI visionary.” It tries to make you the most useful kind of AI‑fluent teammate:

- The one who can turn “we should use AI for this” into a specific, testable experiment.
- The one who can say “yes, but with these guardrails.”
- The one who can say “no” when a shiny idea is going to create a slow‑motion ethics fiasco.

We’ll do that by:

- Giving you **role‑specific playbooks** (PO/PM, SM/Coach, BA/PM, etc.).
- Teaching you how to **design AI‑augmented workflows and agents** using simple canvases.
- Helping you **build habits and rituals** so AI fluency sticks at team and personal levels.
- Offering **toolkits** you can copy into your own Google Drive and actually use.

You can read this cover‑to‑cover, but you don’t have to. You can also:

- Flip to your role’s chapter and start there.
- Steal one template, run one 30‑day experiment, and see what happens.
- Use the ethics checklists when you get that “uh oh, is this a problem?” feeling.

You don’t need permission to start. You don’t need a perfect strategy. You don’t need to announce anything on LinkedIn.

You just need to be willing to treat AI like you treat everything else in product work:

- Start where you are.
- Make your assumptions explicit.
- Run small experiments.
- Reflect and adapt.

The rest of this book shows you how.
